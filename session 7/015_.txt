Event flow in the DOM describes how an event travels through the document when something is triggered, such as a click. It has three phases: capturing phase (top → target), target phase (on the element), and bubbling phase (target → top).

In event bubbling, an event triggered on a child element also propagates up to its parent elements. For example, in a Flipkart-style shopping cart, clicking the “Add to Cart” button inside a product card could also trigger a click handler on the entire product card or container if bubbling is not properly managed, potentially causing unintended actions like opening the product page or duplicating cart updates.
